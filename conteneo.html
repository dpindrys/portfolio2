Overview

The Problem

The existing Conteneo website was confusing, painfully off brand, and would be a horrible vehicle to announce Weave: a massive redesign of their flagship platform.

The Solution

Conteneo needed to communicate their true brand more clearly and consistently, and improve the information architecture of their website.

My Contribution

Since I had joined Conteneo, I had been working with the team to find Conteneo's voice. With an idea of how to visually convey their personality and offerings, while focusing on establishing a new and cleaner information architecture, I designed and developed a website running on WordPress to announce the redesign of Weave.

My Role

Designer, Developer

Timeline

2 weeks in March 2017

Team Members

Luke Hohmann (content), Laura Richardson (content), Dan O'Leary (deployment)

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Sublime Press, WordPress

Finding Conteneo's Voice

Conteneo's existing website was all business and stock photos, and didn't explain their software clearly.

Seeing what sticks

Internally, Conteneo used Star Trek quotes as humorous ways to talk about it's product. Along with getting an idea the aesthetic the founder was envisioning (clean, modern, bold) I threw some designs up on the wall.

A few people on the team really liked of these ideas, but none really stuck, especially with the founder.

Finding the Core

Leaning more on what I saw as the personality traits that were at the core of who Conteneo was (friendly, social, colorful, hard working) I set out looking for some inspiration.

I found some illustrations on a stock photo website that really spoke to me, and thought they might have some wheels. I paid the small fee and started plucking out the people to rearrange and use on the Conteneo website.

Speaking clearly about Weave

Now that I had all these wonderful people, why not turn them into users of Weave? Since Weave was a digital representation of their in-person forums, Innovation Games, it seemed like second nature to arrange these people around screenshots of Weave forums.

Describing the components and principles of Weave

Taking the metaphor a step further, we could use the "users" to help describe complex ideas of Weave, such as the idea that collaboration is best between multiple small teams and describing the Guest List component.

Designing and developing Conteneo.co

Since I had about 2 weeks until launch, I went right into design and developing the website. I found a WordPress port to Zurb's Foundation framework (the one that this very site is running on) that I could use to quickly make a custom WordPress theme that the team could edit the content of.

Flat but deep

Conteneo's previous site was a catacomb of content hidden behind a bajillion dropdown menus. After a little thinking and testing with the team, I decided on a tabular system with anchor links down the side of each page.

This allowed for the loads of content Conteneo wanted to keep, kept it pretty transparent and unhidden, and allowed it to be scalable and ready to add more content to.
