Overview

The Problem

Users were complaining about not being able to find their assets, and Conteneo was noticing a lack of engagement. I observed users getting stuck and not being able to complete their goals.

The Solution

Based on user feedback, Conteneo hired me to redesign their platform from the ground up. The team and I made Weave more transparent, easier to get started on, more social and more engaging, and more fun to explore and use.

My Contribution

I acted as the user's advocate, helping the team balance business goals and technological constraints and with usability concerns. With the PM, I synthesized the user research findings in to a storymap that helped plan future feature releases. Leading development, I designed, iterated, and defined the user flows by designing and sharing interactive prototypes with the development team and designed all user interface elements. I crafted the HTML & CSS for the wen app, working with a front end developer to integrate javascript and hook-up to the back end.

User Research

Mental Models

We asked users to how Weave could look as a physical space to give us an idea of how they see themselves navigating it.

We learned that they saw the system as a series of individual spaces: different spaces for different forum states, frameworks, framework assets, projects, etc.

Need Finding

By eliciting their complaints and requests of our users and observing them interact with the current platform, we discovered that users:
  • Thought the dashboard was confusing
  • Wanted larger backgrounds in forums
  • Thought each framework should have a "home page"
  • Want more control over their forums
  • Wanted to know who is in the forum with them

Ideas for a new Idea Engine

Based on user feedback, my own hueristic evaluation and stakeholder requirements, I started the redesign of Weave's forums by quickly doing a few sketches and low fidelity wireframes to visualize:
  • Player presence
  • Full screen canvas
  • Mini-map navigation
  • Ability to create new item types in-forum

User Testing a Prototype

From these, I made a prototype with Keynote for creating a framework and starting a forum with a group of Conteneo's users. We learned a few things while watching them tap through the prototype:
  • Beginning with a large plus sign didn't give enough context to know what to do first.
  • Chat is very important and should never be hidden
  • A mini-map just got in the way
  • They liked being able to define regions on the image with a pen tool, but wanted something quicker.

Making Forums Social Again

The Problem
Forums, at one time practiced on a wall with people in the same room, lacked the same sense of social engagement as they did when they happened in person.

The Solution
By giving users awareness of who is with them in a forum, when new participants join or leave, and by providing a more intuitive and easier-to-use method for inviting others, forums can now play out what they're intended to: as engaging, collaborative, social business meeting.

Forums, Front and Center

The Problem
Users complained about not being able to find their forums in the existing platform's deep and opaque hierarchy.

The Solution
Flattening the hierarchy and providing a dashboard with the most recent forums in each state: running, upcoming, and completed, helped users to effortlessly find the forum they're looking for.

Control, without Diving In

The Problem
Sometimes users wanted to see info about the status of their forum or invite new participants, but didn't want to join the forum to do so.

The Solution
On a forum's info page, one can see the status, properties, facilitator and participants of the forum, and send invites to join.
